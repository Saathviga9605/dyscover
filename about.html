What is Dyslexia?

Dyslexia is a learning disability that primarily affects reading and related language-based skills. It's characterized by difficulties with accurate and fluent word recognition, poor spelling, and related difficulties with reading comprehension.

Dyslexia is like trying to play a word game where the letters dance around and never stay still. Imagine reading a sentence, but the words keep jumbling or flipping. That’s how it feels to someone with dyslexia—frustrating, confusing, but not impossible!

Common Symptoms

  • Mixing up letters and numbers
  • Trouble reading out loud
  • Writing letters backward (like b and d)
  • Difficulty following directions
  • Struggling with spelling

What Causes It?

Dyslexia is primarily believed to stem from differences in how the brain processes language and reading. While the exact cause remains unknown, research suggests a strong genetic component, with dyslexia often running in families. Differences in brain structure and function related to language processing are also thought to play a role. Additionally, risk factors like low birth weight, premature birth, and exposure to certain substances during gestation can increase the likelihood of developing dyslexia.

Dyslexia isn’t about being lazy or not trying. It’s a brain-based difference in how people process language. It can run in families and sometimes just happens randomly. But with the right tools and support, every dyslexic child can thrive.
